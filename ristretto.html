Ristretto Recipe

Ingredients

  • 18-20 grams of finely ground espresso coffee
  • 30 ml (1 ounce) of hot water
  • Optional: Sweetener (sugar or syrup) to taste

Instructions

  1. Grind your coffee beans finely, similar to the grind you would use for a regular espresso.
  2. Set up your espresso machine with a portafilter and tamp the ground coffee evenly into the basket. Make sure the coffee is packed tightly.
  3. Pull a shot of espresso with a shorter extraction time (around 20-25 seconds). This will produce a ristretto, which is a more concentrated shot of espresso.
  4. The result should be about 30 ml (1 ounce) of coffee, which is less than the usual 60 ml (2 ounces) for a standard espresso shot, but with a more intense, richer flavor.
  5. If desired, you can add a small amount of sweetener to balance the bitterness, though many enjoy ristretto in its pure form.
  6. Serve immediately in a small espresso cup and enjoy the concentrated, bold flavor of the ristretto!

  • Since ristretto is a shorter extraction, itâ€™s more concentrated than a regular espresso and has a fuller, richer flavor with a slightly sweeter profile.
  • If you like your ristretto less intense, you can experiment with adjusting the amount of water or using a slightly coarser grind.
  • If you're using a manual espresso machine or a stovetop espresso maker, the same principle applies: reduce the water used and extract a smaller, more concentrated shot.

Pro Tip: Ristretto is perfect for those who enjoy a stronger, bolder coffee flavor but without the bitterness that can sometimes come from a regular espresso shot. It's also a great base for other drinks like lattes or cappuccinos when you want a more intense flavor.
