Macchiato Recipe

Ingredients

  • 1 shot of espresso (18-20 grams of coffee)
  • 1-2 teaspoons of steamed milk (for a traditional macchiato)
  • Optional: Sweetener (sugar or syrups) for added flavor

Instructions

  1. Grind the coffee beans finely and brew a shot of espresso (about 30 ml).
  2. Steam a small amount of milk (1-2 teaspoons) until it's frothy, but not too much foam. Traditional macchiatos have a small amount of milk, just enough to "mark" the espresso.
  3. Pour the espresso into a small, pre-warmed cup.
  4. Using a spoon, carefully add the steamed milk to the center of the espresso. The milk should form a small spot (or "mark") on the espresso, which gives the drink its name ("macchiato" means "stained" or "marked" in Italian).
  5. Optional: Add sweetener (sugar, vanilla syrup) if you prefer a sweeter taste. Stir gently if desired.
  6. Serve immediately and enjoy your macchiato!

Tips

  • A traditional macchiato has very little milk, so if you prefer more milk, consider trying a **latte macchiato**, where the milk is the base, and the espresso is added on top.
  • For a stronger coffee flavor, opt for a single shot of espresso. If you want a milder drink, use a double shot.
  • To make a **caramel macchiato**, drizzle caramel syrup over the drink for added sweetness and flavor.

Pro Tip: If you're using a milk frother, ensure you're only frothing a small amount of milk to keep it light and airy. The key to a perfect macchiato is the balance between the strong espresso and the small amount of milk.
