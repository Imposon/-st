Irish Coffee Recipe

Ingredients

  • 1 cup (240 ml) of hot brewed coffee (preferably strong)
  • 1 shot (45 ml) of Irish whiskey
  • 1-2 teaspoons of brown sugar (or to taste)
  • Fresh whipped cream (for topping)

Instructions

  1. Brew a cup of strong, hot coffee. It’s important to use freshly brewed coffee for the best flavor.
  2. While the coffee is brewing, pre-warm your mug by filling it with hot water and letting it sit for a minute or so, then discarding the water.
  3. Add 1-2 teaspoons of brown sugar to the warmed mug, then pour in the hot coffee. Stir until the sugar is dissolved.
  4. Add 1 shot (45 ml) of Irish whiskey to the coffee and stir to combine.
  5. Top the coffee with a generous dollop of fresh whipped cream. The cream should float on top, creating a layered effect.
  6. Optional: Garnish with a light dusting of cocoa powder or a few coffee beans on top of the whipped cream for a nice presentation.
  7. Serve immediately and enjoy your Irish Coffee!

Tips

  • For the best taste, use freshly brewed coffee and high-quality Irish whiskey such as Jameson or Bushmills.
  • You can adjust the sweetness by adding more or less brown sugar, or even using a flavored syrup if you prefer.
  • If you don’t have fresh whipped cream, you can use store-bought whipped cream, but fresh cream will give the drink a richer texture.

Pro Tip: When making the whipped cream, use heavy cream and whisk it until soft peaks form. Don’t over-whip, as it can turn into butter.
