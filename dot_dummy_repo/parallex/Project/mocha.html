Mocha

Ingredients

  • 1 shot of espresso (18-20 grams of coffee)
  • 1 tablespoon of cocoa powder or 2 tablespoons of chocolate syrup
  • 240 ml (8 oz) of steamed milk
  • Optional: Sweetener (sugar, vanilla syrup, or flavored syrups)
  • Whipped cream (for topping, optional)
  • Chocolate shavings or cocoa powder (for garnish, optional)

Instructions

  1. Grind the coffee beans finely and brew a shot of espresso (about 30 ml).
  2. Heat the milk in a steam wand or stovetop until it reaches around 65-70°C (149-158°F) and becomes creamy and frothy.
  3. If using cocoa powder, mix it with a little hot water to create a smooth chocolate paste. If using chocolate syrup, you can skip this step.
  4. Pour the brewed espresso into a warm cup or mug.
  5. Add the chocolate paste or syrup to the espresso and stir until well combined.
  6. Pour the steamed milk into the espresso-chocolate mixture, leaving some space at the top for foam.
  7. Top with a dollop of whipped cream if desired and garnish with chocolate shavings or a sprinkle of cocoa powder.
  8. Optional: Add sweetener (sugar, vanilla syrup, or any other flavoring) to taste, and stir gently.
  9. Serve immediately and enjoy your mocha!

Tips

  • If you prefer a stronger chocolate flavor, increase the amount of cocoa powder or syrup to taste.
  • For a creamier mocha, use whole milk or a milk alternative like oat or almond milk.
  • If you don't have a steam wand, you can froth the milk with a milk frother or by shaking heated milk in a jar.
  • For a **white mocha**, substitute the cocoa powder or chocolate syrup with white chocolate syrup.

Pro Tip: To make your mocha extra indulgent, add a drizzle of chocolate syrup inside the cup before pouring in the espresso. This will give you a beautiful chocolatey swirl and extra flavor with each sip!
