Filter Coffee Recipe

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons of ground coffee (preferably filter coffee blend, medium grind)
  • 1 cup (240 ml) of water
  • Optional: Milk (dairy or plant-based, to taste)
  • Optional: Sugar (to taste)

Instructions

  1. Heat the water to just below boiling point, about 200°F (93°C). It’s important not to use boiling water, as it can extract bitterness from the coffee grounds.
  2. Place a filter paper in your coffee filter or pour-over setup (like a V60 or Chemex). If you’re using a metal filter (such as a South Indian filter), no paper filter is needed.
  3. Add 2 tablespoons of ground coffee to the filter. Make sure the coffee is evenly distributed for a uniform extraction.
  4. Slowly pour the hot water over the coffee grounds in a circular motion. Pour in small increments, allowing the water to soak and drip through. The total brewing time should be around 4-5 minutes.
  5. Once the water has dripped through the filter and the coffee has brewed, remove the filter. Your fresh filter coffee is now ready!
  6. If you prefer your coffee black, pour it into a cup and enjoy as is. Otherwise, add milk and sugar to taste.

Tips

  • Use freshly ground coffee for the best flavor. The grind should be medium, similar to the grind used for drip coffee.
  • If you don’t have a coffee filter, you can use a fine mesh strainer or make a DIY filter with a clean cloth, though a dedicated filter is preferred for best results.
  • To achieve a stronger cup of coffee, use more ground coffee (about 1 tablespoon more per cup) or reduce the amount of water.
  • If you're preparing coffee for a group, you can brew several cups in a large drip coffee maker or use a traditional South Indian coffee filter.

Pro Tip: For extra smoothness and flavor, try brewing your filter coffee in a South Indian coffee filter. The coffee drips through slowly, resulting in a rich, full-bodied cup of coffee.
