Latte Recipe

Ingredients

  • 1 shot of espresso (18-20 grams of coffee)
  • 240 ml (about 8 oz) of fresh whole milk
  • Optional: Sweetener (sugar, honey, or flavored syrups)

Instructions

  1. Grind the coffee beans finely and brew a shot of espresso (about 30 ml).
  2. Steam the milk using a steam wand until it reaches 65-70°C (149-158°F), creating a creamy texture with a light foam on top.
  3. Pour the shot of espresso into a warm cup.
  4. Slowly pour the steamed milk into the espresso, holding back the foam with a spoon. Then, spoon the foam on top to finish the latte.
  5. Optional: Add sweetener (sugar, honey, or flavored syrups) and stir gently.
  6. Serve immediately and enjoy!

Tips

  • For a creamier latte, use whole milk or a milk alternative like oat milk for a similar texture.
  • Make sure to steam the milk to the right temperature (65-70°C or 149-158°F) to get the perfect texture—too hot and it will taste burnt, too cold and the foam won't form.
  • If you prefer a stronger coffee flavor, try adding an extra shot of espresso (double shot) or using a darker roast coffee.
  • Latte art can be created by pouring the steamed milk in a controlled, swirling motion. Practice makes perfect!

Pro Tip: To create beautiful latte art, practice pouring the milk in a steady, controlled manner. Start with a steady stream and finish with a swirl to create hearts, rosettas, or tulip shapes.
