Ingredients

  • 1-2 tablespoons of freshly ground coffee (or 1 coffee pod for single-serve machines)
  • 1 cup (240 ml) of water
  • Optional: Ice (for iced black coffee)

Instructions

  1. Measure out 1-2 tablespoons of freshly ground coffee. Use a medium to coarse grind, depending on your brewing method. For a stronger coffee, you can use more coffee grounds.
  2. Heat 1 cup (240 ml) of water. The ideal temperature for brewing black coffee is between 195°F (90°C) and 205°F (96°C), just below boiling.
  3. Brewing methods can vary, so choose one based on your preference:
    • Drip coffee maker: Add the coffee grounds to the filter and pour hot water over the grounds.
    • French press: Add the coffee grounds to the press, pour hot water over the grounds, stir, place the lid on, and let steep for 4 minutes before pressing the plunger.
    • Pour-over: Place the coffee grounds in a filter, then slowly pour the hot water over the grounds in a circular motion, allowing the coffee to drip through.
    • Espresso machine: Add the coffee grounds to the portafilter, tamp them down, and pull a shot of espresso.
  4. Once brewed, serve immediately. If you prefer your coffee black, do not add milk or cream.
  5. Optional: If you like iced black coffee, pour the brewed coffee over ice in a glass. You can chill the coffee beforehand for a smoother experience.

Tips

  • Use freshly ground coffee beans for the best flavor. The grind size should match your brewing method (coarser for French press, medium for drip coffee, fine for espresso).
  • If you prefer a smoother cup, try using filtered water to brew your coffee.
  • If the coffee tastes too bitter, experiment with reducing the brewing time or adjusting the coffee-to-water ratio to suit your taste.
  • For iced black coffee, brew the coffee in advance and refrigerate it, or pour it over ice immediately after brewing.

Pro Tip: If you find black coffee too strong, try adding a pinch of salt to the coffee grounds before brewing. It can help reduce bitterness and enhance the coffee’s natural flavors.
