Turkish Coffee Recipe

Ingredients

  • 1-2 teaspoons of finely ground Turkish coffee (per serving)
  • 1 cup (120 ml) of cold water (per serving)
  • Optional: Sugar (to taste, usually 1-2 teaspoons per cup)
  • Optional: Cardamom or spices (for added flavor)

Instructions

  1. Start by measuring your water. For each cup of Turkish coffee, use one small coffee cup of cold water (around 120 ml).
  2. Pour the cold water into a **cezve** (a small, long-handled pot traditionally used for making Turkish coffee). This is the key vessel used to prepare Turkish coffee.
  3. Add 1-2 teaspoons of finely ground Turkish coffee to the water (depending on how strong you want your coffee).
  4. If you prefer sweetened coffee, add sugar at this stage. Turkish coffee can be made in three levels of sweetness:
    • Sade (unsweetened) – no sugar
    • Az şekerli (lightly sweet) – 1 teaspoon of sugar
    • Orta (medium sweet) – 2 teaspoons of sugar
    • Şekerli (sweet) – 3 teaspoons of sugar
  5. If you like, add a pinch of ground cardamom or other spices for extra flavor. This is an optional addition, but it's common in some variations of Turkish coffee.
  6. Place the cezve over low heat. Slowly heat the mixture, stirring gently until the coffee and sugar (if used) dissolve. Do not stir once it starts heating up.
  7. As the coffee heats, foam will begin to form on top. Just before it begins to boil, remove the cezve from the heat. Let the foam settle for a few seconds, then return it to the heat.
  8. Repeat this process 2-3 times to build the foam. The more foam, the better!
  9. Once the coffee is ready, pour it carefully into small coffee cups, making sure to distribute the foam evenly.
  10. Serve immediately and enjoy your rich, aromatic Turkish coffee!

Tips

  • For the best flavor, always use freshly ground Turkish coffee, as it has a much finer grind than regular coffee.
  • When making Turkish coffee, it’s important to heat it slowly. Never let the coffee come to a rapid boil, as this can cause it to lose its flavor and become bitter.
  • Turkish coffee is traditionally served with a glass of water on the side to cleanse the palate before drinking the coffee.

Pro Tip: Turkish coffee is traditionally served without milk or cream, and it is enjoyed slowly. To enhance the experience, pair it with a piece of Turkish delight or a small sweet treat!
