Ingredients

  • 1 cup of cold water
  • 1-2 tablespoons of finely ground Turkish coffee
  • 1-2 teaspoons of sugar (optional, adjust to taste)
  • Cardamom (optional, for a traditional touch)

Instructions

  1. Measure the water using the coffee cup youâ€™ll be serving in, and pour it into a cezve (Turkish coffee pot).
  2. For each cup of water, add 1-2 heaping teaspoons of finely ground Turkish coffee. Add sugar if desired, adjusting to your taste (1 teaspoon for medium, 2 teaspoons for sweet).
  3. If desired, add a pinch of ground cardamom for extra flavor.
  4. Stir the mixture gently to combine the coffee and water (and sugar/cardamom if using). Do not stir once the coffee starts heating.
  5. Place the cezve over low heat and allow it to heat slowly. As it warms, foam will begin to form on top.
  6. Once the coffee begins to rise (after a few minutes), remove the cezve from the heat. Skim off the foam with a spoon and place it in your coffee cup.
  7. Return the cezve to the heat and allow it to foam up again. Repeat this process 2-3 times to ensure a rich foam.
  8. After the third foam rise, pour the coffee slowly into your cup, allowing the grounds to settle at the bottom. Avoid stirring the coffee once it's in the cup.
  9. Serve immediately and enjoy your traditional Turkish coffee!

Tips

  • For best results, use freshly ground Turkish coffee. The grind should be very fine, almost like powdered sugar.
  • If you're using sugar, be sure to add it while the coffee is cold so it dissolves evenly.
  • Turkish coffee is often served with a glass of water and sometimes with Turkish delight as a sweet treat.
  • If you prefer a stronger brew, you can add more coffee or reduce the water slightly.

Pro Tip: To enhance the flavor, you can experiment by adding a tiny pinch of ground cardamom or even a bit of cinnamon to the coffee grounds before brewing.
